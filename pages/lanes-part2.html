Finding Lane Lines (Part 2)

May 2017

This is an overview of the fourth project of the Udacity nanodegreee. In this project, we try to improve on the approach developed here to find more precise lanes, and can help infer a streering angle (i.e. the radius of the curvature and the position in the lane. The final result can be seen below:


There are xx main steps of the image pipeline:

  • Compute the camera calibration matrix and distortion coefficients given a set of chessboard images.
  • Apply a distortion correction to raw images.
  • Use color transforms, gradients, etc., to create a thresholded binary image.
  • Apply a perspective transform to rectify binary image ("birds-eye view").
  • Detect lane pixels and fit to find the lane boundary.
  • Determine the curvature of the lane and vehicle position with respect to center.
  • Warp the detected lane boundaries back onto the original image.
  • Output visual display of the lane boundaries and numerical estimation of lane curvature and vehicle position

If the lane turns too much, the current solution does not work. Possible improvements would be to:

  • limit the angles of the Hough Lines
  • do the regression at the end of the canny edge step directly
  • use the continuity of directions to stabilize the algorithm
    • for each image, we calculate the lines, together with a confidence probability (longer lines result in higher confidence)
    • At time t, the ouput lines are given by average the lines of Image t, t-1 and t-2 (for instance) weighted by the confidence and other weights
A more thorough solution to that problem will be built later in the degree
